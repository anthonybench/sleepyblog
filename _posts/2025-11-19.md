This experiment is finally over. Key conclusions:

    - Building a house is hard (someone should've told me!).
    - Ignoring raw land work such as survey, forestry and utilities, 2 middle class earners can have a brand new custom designed home as long as they're willing to learn EVERYTHING and do all physically viable work, without substantial savings.
    - Having working plumbing fixtures & appliances in your area of residence is wildly underrated.
    - For landscaping at scale, you need heavy equipment, tractor at minimum. The amount of time it takes to manually dig out stumps, utility trenches, and move dirt manually cripples your ability to do anything else. Bad news is I have to buy a tractor, good news is I get to buy a tractor.

Of the things I've never done before, the most entertaining things were:

    - Soffit vent baffling.
    - The plumbing and electric for the well house. Figuring out how well systems work was genuinely enlightening.
    - Making an arch.
    - Bull nose drywall corners.

And the things I'd not before that were very much _not_ entertaining:

    - Crawlspace insulation
    - Drywall sanding for a smooth finish (_*shudders_)
